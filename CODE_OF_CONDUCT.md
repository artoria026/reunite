## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open, welcoming, and safe environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in Reunite a harassment-free experience for everyone.

This project exists to help people during emotionally difficult moments. **Empathy, respect, and kindness are not optional here.**

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

* Being respectful, patient, and kind
* Showing empathy toward other community members
* Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
* Focusing on what is best for the community and the project
* Using inclusive and welcoming language

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* Harassment, discrimination, or hate speech of any kind
* Personal attacks, insults, or demeaning comments
* Trolling, flame wars, or deliberate disruption
* Publishing private information without consent
* Any behavior that puts people or animals at risk

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

* All project spaces (GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions)
* All forms of communication related to Reunite
* Any situation where an individual is representing the project

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to:

* Remove, edit, or reject contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
* Temporarily or permanently ban contributors for unacceptable behavior

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it privately to the project maintainers.

Reports will be handled with discretion and care.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these principles when responding to violations:

* Listen carefully
* Act fairly and consistently
* Prioritize safety and well-being
* Protect vulnerable individuals

Consequences may range from a warning to permanent removal from the project, depending on severity.
